# Case Study Skill

A case study is proof. Not a success story. Not a testimonial. Proof that ENAC's approach worked in a specific situation with a specific result.

---

## What Makes a Case Study Credible

**Specific, not generic.** Named client (or named type if confidential). Named challenge. Named outcome with numbers.

**"We've done this before" is not proof. "$1 billion in two years" is proof.**

---

## Structure

### 1. The Situation (1-2 paragraphs)
What was actually happening. Not the polished version: the real one.
Include: the constraint, the tension, what had already been tried and failed.

### 2. The Insight (1 paragraph)
What ENAC saw that others missed. The reframe that changed the approach.
This is the most differentiating section. If it sounds generic, it is.

### 3. The Work (1-2 paragraphs)
What the engagement looked like. Which phases of the methodology. Who was in the room.
Name the Confront/Co-Create/Copilot phases explicitly.

### 4. The Outcome (1 paragraph)
Specific. Numbers where possible. Named results.
Include: what changed, what was produced, what happened next.

### 5. What This Means (1 paragraph, optional)
The principle the case study illustrates. One sentence that makes it applicable to the next client.

---

## The Insight Test

Read only the Insight section. Ask: could this be applied to any client in this industry?

If yes: it's not a real insight. A real insight is specific to this client's situation.

---

## Numbers and Evidence

Every case study should include at least one:
- Dollar figure (revenue, cost, investment)
- Percentage (improvement, reduction, growth)
- Time metric (faster, shorter cycle, earlier delivery)
- Named artifact (a decision, a product, a process that now exists)

If none of these exist: the outcome section is not done.

---

## Confidentiality Handling

If the client cannot be named:
- Use the industry and company size: "a mid-market retailer" or "a federal agency"
- Keep all specific outcomes: those are the proof
- Do not sanitize the challenge: vague situations make vague case studies

---

## Voice Rules

- Past tense throughout
- Active voice: "We ran 12 executive interviews" not "Executive interviews were conducted"
- No marketing superlatives: no "transformative," "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- Short paragraphs: 3-4 sentences maximum
- No em-dashes: use commas, semicolons, colons, or restructure

---

## Quality Tests

| Test | Question | If No |
|------|----------|-------|
| Specific | Is there a real number or named result? | Add proof |
| Insight | Is the reframe specific to this client? | Rewrite it |
| Voice | Does it sound like ENAC talking, not marketing? | Remove superlatives |
| Challenge | Is the real challenge named, not a polished version? | Be more honest |
| Methodology | Are the three phases visible? | Make them explicit |

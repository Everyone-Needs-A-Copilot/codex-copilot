# Proposal Response Skill

A proposal is a commercial argument. Not a capabilities overview. Not a methodology brochure. An argument for why ENAC is the right partner for this specific engagement.

---

## The Core Test

Before writing anything, answer: **What is the specific argument this proposal makes?**

If the answer is "we're experienced and have a good methodology": start over. That's not an argument.

A real argument: "The reason this hasn't worked before is X. We approach it differently because Y. Here's what that produced for a client in a similar situation."

---

## Structure for a Proposal Deck (Figma Slides)

Use the presentation narrative arc. The proposal IS a presentation.

1. **Cover**: Name the real problem, not the engagement
2. **Understanding**: Show you've diagnosed their situation, not just heard it
3. **The Reframe**: The insight that changes how they see their problem
4. **Approach**: The three phases: Confront, Co-Create, Copilot
5. **Proof**: Specific prior work with results
6. **Investment**: The number, clearly stated
7. **Next Step**: One specific action

Never include: agenda slides, "About ENAC" as slide 2, methodology slides that could apply to any client.

---

## Structure for a Proposal Document (Markdown -> Word)

1. **Executive Summary**: The argument in 2 paragraphs. If they read nothing else, they read this.
2. **Our Understanding**: Their situation in their language. Not a problem statement.
3. **Approach**: Confront/Co-Create/Copilot with specific activities per phase.
4. **Deliverables**: What exists at the end of each phase. Specific.
5. **Investment**: Dollar amount, payment schedule, what is and is not included.
6. **Why Now**: The cost of not acting. Make it concrete.
7. **Next Steps**: One clear action.

## Responding to an RFP

RFPs have requirements. Address them. Then make the argument.

**Required sections:** Answer what they asked for. Do not skip or reframe requirements as irrelevant.

**Differentiating sections:** After requirements, make the ENAC argument. Don't just check the boxes.

**Language:** Use their terms from the RFP. If they call it "discovery," call it discovery: then show how Confront is different from what they mean by discovery.

---

## What to Exclude

- Generic methodology slides that could come from any firm
- Long biography sections for team members
- "Why ENAC" slides that list awards or years in business
- Nested bullets explaining the process in detail
- Any slide that exists to fill space

Every slide, every section must carry part of the argument.

---

## Investment Section Rules

Same as SOW rules:
- State the number clearly. Never hedge or bury it.
- Justify in terms of value to them, not effort from us
- "This is what focused looks like" not "This represents X hours"
- Payment schedule: simple, clear, linked to milestones not calendar months

## Quality Tests

| Test | Question | If No |
|------|----------|-------|
| Argument | Does the proposal make a specific argument? | Rewrite the executive summary |
| Their situation | Would the client recognize themselves in the Understanding? | Rewrite it |
| Proof | Is there a specific result with a number? | Add proof |
| Differentiation | Is there something here no other firm would say? | Find the ENAC angle |
| Investment clarity | Is the number stated clearly and early? | Move it up |
| No jargon | Zero banned words? | Remove them |
